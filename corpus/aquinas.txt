To love is to will the good of another. True love is not based on what we can receive, but on what we can give. In willing the good of others, we participate in the very love of God, who wills the existence and goodness of every creature.

Three things are necessary for the salvation of man: to know what he ought to believe; to know what he ought to desire; and to know what he ought to do. Faith, hope, and charity guide us in these three paths, drawing us closer to our ultimate end, which is union with God.

Mercy is the greatest of virtues in relation to our neighbor. It is the hallmark of the Christian life, for it is through mercy that we imitate the compassion of our Heavenly Father. When we show mercy, we do not diminish justice, but fulfill it in love.

Faith has to do with things that are not seen, and hope with things that are not at hand. We walk by faith and not by sight in this life, trusting that the promises of God will be realized in the next. Our restlessness is quieted when we anchor our hope in the eternal.
