Listen carefully, my son, to the master's instructions, and attend to them with the ear of your heart. This is the advice of a father who loves you. Welcome it, and faithfully put it into practice, so that by the labor of obedience you may return to Him from whom you had departed by the sloth of disobedience.

Prefer nothing whatever to Christ, and may He bring us all together to everlasting life. Let us keep our eyes fixed on Him who has called us, and run with a warm and eager heart in the way of God's commandments. In this school of the Lord's service, we hope to never depart from His guidance.

Idleness is the enemy of the soul. Therefore, the brothers should have specified periods for manual labor as well as for prayerful reading. By work, we sustain ourselves and participate in the creation, and by reading, we nourish our minds and hearts with the word of God. Let all things be done with moderation.

Let all guests who arrive be received like Christ, for He will say: I was a stranger and you welcomed me. Show them every kindness and hospitality, praying with them and sharing with them the peace of the monastery. In receiving guests, we receive Christ Himself, who comes to us in the guise of the poor and the traveler.
