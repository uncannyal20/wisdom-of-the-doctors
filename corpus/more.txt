Give me Thy grace, good Lord, to set the world at naught; to set my mind fast upon Thee, and not to hang upon the blast of men's mouths. To be content to be solitary. Not to long for worldly company, but to labor to bring my mind fast unto God, and to make Him my best friend.

You must not abandon the ship in a storm because you cannot control the winds. What you cannot turn to good, you must at least make as little bad as you can. For it is not possible that all things should be well, unless all men were good, which I think will not be for these many years.

Nothing can come but that which God wills. And I am very sure that whatsoever that be, seem it never so bad in our eyes, it shall indeed be the best. He will either keep us from temptation or give us the strength to overcome it, if we place our trust fully in His hands.

To live well in the world, we must enjoy the gifts of God with a clean heart, and never let them steal our love from Him. For the ordinary things of life—our family, our friends, a simple meal, a book, a laugh—are all graces given to us by our Creator to sweeten our earthly pilgrimage.
