Works Cited & Library

St. Francis de Sales

Introduction to the Devout Life
Published in 1609, this spiritual classic is a guide for laypeople seeking holiness in everyday life. St. Francis de Sales emphasizes that devotion is compatible with every vocation and profession, teaching that God calls us to sanctify our ordinary duties.
Treatise on the Love of God
Written as a deeper sequel to his first book, this profound work details the nature, origin, growth, and practice of divine love in the human soul, showing how divine love is the source of all virtues.
Consoling Thoughts
A curated collection of warm, encouraging excerpts focusing on finding inward peace, trusting in divine Providence, and maintaining gentleness with one's own faults and imperfections.
Letters of Spiritual Direction
Personal correspondence from St. Francis de Sales offering practical, gentle, and custom-tailored pastoral advice to laypeople and religious under his spiritual care.

St. Augustine of Hippo

Confessions
Written around AD 397–400, this autobiographical work describes St. Augustine's restless youth, philosophical struggles, and ultimate conversion to Christianity. It is widely considered one of the greatest spiritual diaries in human history.
The City of God
A cornerstone of Western thought, this masterpiece contrasts the "earthly city" (driven by self-love) with the "heavenly city" (driven by love of God) as it defends Christianity against accusations following the sack of Rome.
De Doctrina Christiana (On Christian Doctrine)
A foundational theological treatise detailing how to study, interpret, and teach the Scriptures, laying down rules for biblical hermeneutics and Christian rhetoric.
Sermons on the Psalms & Sermons
A vast collection of pastoral and mystical homilies delivered by St. Augustine over decades of active ministry, explaining Scripture and the Psalms to his congregation in Hippo.

St. Thérèse of Lisieux

Story of a Soul
The autobiographical manuscripts of St. Thérèse, published posthumously in 1898. It describes her "Little Way" of spiritual childhood—a path of absolute trust, small daily sacrifices, and surrender to God's merciful love.
Last Conversations
A collection of St. Thérèse's final words, observations, and spiritual instructions recorded on her deathbed by her sister novices, reflecting her profound peace during extreme suffering.
Letters to Céline
Intimate and encouraging spiritual letters written by Thérèse to her sister Céline, outlining the practice of humility, seeking hidden virtue, and loving Jesus in the ordinary.

St. Thomas Aquinas

Summa Theologiae
Written 1265–1274, this monumental work synthesizes Christian theology with Aristotelian philosophy. It covers the existence of God (the "Five Ways"), the nature of virtue, grace, and Christ, forming the backbone of Catholic scholastic thought.

St. Alphonsus Liguori

The Way of Salvation and of Perfection
A collection of spiritual reflections and meditations on the eternal truths, the practice of prayer, and conforming our human will to the divine will in all circumstances.

St. Benedict of Nursia

Rule of Saint Benedict
Written in the 6th century, this book of precepts has guided Western monasticism for over a millennium. It establishes a balanced life of prayer (Ora), work (Labora), silence, humility, and hospitality.

St. Thomas More

Utopia & Tower Letters
Known for his political philosophy and firm martyrdom, St. Thomas More's writings include "Utopia" (1516), detailing an ideal island society, and his letters written from the Tower of London while awaiting execution, reflecting deep trust in God amidst suffering.

St. Teresa of Ávila

The Interior Castle & Way of Perfection
Classic works of mystical theology and spiritual instruction. St. Teresa of Ávila guides the soul through the seven mansions of prayer toward spiritual marriage with Christ, emphasizing vocal, meditative, and contemplative prayer.

St. Louis de Montfort

True Devotion to Mary & Secret of the Rosary
Foundational texts of Marian spirituality. St. Louis de Montfort presents a path of total consecration to Jesus through Mary, explaining how Mary leads souls directly to her Son, and details the mysteries of the Holy Rosary.
